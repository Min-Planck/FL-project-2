### Abstract
The rapid growth of encrypted network traffic in recent years have presented a significant challenges for the task of traffic classification. Traditional machine learning approaches, which usually assumes that data are centralized, which doesn’t happen often in practical, especially in distributed framework such as multi-domain SDN networks. Collecting and storing data in a centralized controller poses potential challenges such as privacy concerns, communication overhead, and limited computational resources. This paper proposes a Federated Learning (FL)-based method for encrypted traffic classification, enabling each SDN domain to locally train models without sharing raw data. Then a central controller aggregates local models to form a global one, preserving data privacy while improving classification performance. We evaluated our approach on a real-world multi-domain encrypted traffic dataset and compared it with SOTA FL methods. The experimental results demonstrate superior performance in terms of accuracy, convergence speed, and cross-domain generalization. The findings highlight the feasibility and effectiveness of FL in Non-IID scenarios and privacy-sensitive network environments.
